Meeting Transcript — Brainstorm: Restaurant Voting Menu App
Meeting Title: Brainstorm – Interactive Menu App for Restaurant
 Date: March 12, 2026
 Time: 10:00 AM – 10:48 AM
 Participants:
Sarah (Product Manager)
Daniel (Engineering Manager)
Priya (Data & Analytics Manager)
Lucas (UX/UI Manager)

[10:00 AM] Sarah:
 Alright, thanks everyone for joining. So, we have this new stakeholder — they own a restaurant and want to build an app where customers can basically vote on menu items. The idea is to refine the menu over time based on popularity.
[10:01 AM] Daniel:
 Interesting. So this is like a feedback loop directly tied to the menu itself?
[10:02 AM] Sarah:
 Exactly. Customers would rate or vote on dishes, and over time, the restaurant keeps only the highest-performing ones.
[10:03 AM] Priya:
 That’s actually great from a data perspective. If we track ratings over time, we can identify trends, seasonal preferences, even predict demand.
[10:04 AM] Lucas:
 From a UX standpoint, we need to keep it super simple. If voting takes more than one or two taps, people won’t do it.

[10:06 AM] Sarah:
 Right. So maybe a simple thumbs up/down or a 1–5 rating system?
[10:06 AM] Lucas:
 I’d lean toward something visual — like stars or even quick reactions.
[10:07 AM] Daniel:
 We also need to think about authentication. Do users need accounts, or can they vote anonymously?
[10:08 AM] Priya:
 Anonymous might be easier initially, but then we risk spam or unreliable data.

[10:10 AM] Sarah:
 Good point. Maybe lightweight login? Like Google or Apple sign-in?
[10:11 AM] Daniel:
 Yeah, that would work. Keeps friction low but still gives us some structure.

[10:13 AM] Priya:
 Another thing — if the goal is to optimize inventory, we should connect votes with ingredient usage.
[10:14 AM] Sarah:
 Can you expand on that?
[10:14 AM] Priya:
 Sure. If a dish gets high ratings, we can assume higher demand, which means more frequent ingredient purchases. Over time, we could even suggest optimal stock levels.
[10:15 AM] Daniel:
 That would require a backend system tracking ingredients per dish.

[10:17 AM] Sarah:
 Yes, and that brings us to an important constraint: customers should NOT see the ingredients. That’s internal only.
[10:18 AM] Lucas:
 So we basically have two views — customer-facing and internal dashboard?
[10:18 AM] Daniel:
 Exactly. Customer sees menu, ratings, basic info. Staff sees ingredients, inventory, analytics.

[10:20 AM] Lucas:
 For the customer app, I’d suggest:
Home/About
Menu
Contact
Location (with map)
[10:21 AM] Sarah:
 Yes, that matches what the stakeholder requested.

[10:23 AM] Priya:
 For analytics, we should include:
Top-rated dishes
Lowest-performing dishes
Voting trends over time
[10:24 AM] Daniel:
 And maybe alerts — like “this dish is consistently underperforming.”

[10:26 AM] Sarah:
 That’s great. It directly supports their goal of reducing waste.
[10:27 AM] Priya:
 Exactly. Less popular dishes = less stock = cost savings.

[10:30 AM] Lucas:
 One more thing — how do we encourage users to vote?
[10:31 AM] Sarah:
 Good question.
[10:31 AM] Lucas:
 Maybe subtle prompts after viewing a dish or after visiting the restaurant.
[10:32 AM] Daniel:
 We could also add optional notifications, but that might be phase two.

[10:35 AM] Sarah:
 Alright, let’s summarize direction:
Simple voting system
Lightweight authentication
Dual interface (customer vs staff)
Ingredient visibility restricted to staff
Focus on analytics for decision-making

[10:38 AM] Priya:
 And strong data tracking from day one.
[10:39 AM] Daniel:
 Plus scalable backend, in case they expand.

[10:41 AM] Lucas:
 And clean, fast UX — especially for mobile.

[10:45 AM] Sarah:
 Perfect. I’ll consolidate this into initial requirements and we can refine in the next session.
[10:46 AM] Daniel:
 Sounds good.
[10:46 AM] Priya:
 Thanks everyone.
[10:47 AM] Lucas:
 Great session.

[10:48 AM] Meeting Ended
